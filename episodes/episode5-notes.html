  2. Robot Operating System (ROS): middleware that standardizes (many aspects of) writing code for robots. Standards make life easier and let you focus on bigger things. Imagine if US gas stations had a nozzle that was incompatible with your car! I often take for granted that almost everything builds upon some kind of standard.

  3. We speak about computer-controlled, instead of human-controlled, factory machinery. I learned it’s properly known as “numerical control”.

  4. The metric system has been around since the 1700s and was the basis for the International System of Units (SI) that we use today.

  5. We speak about TCP/IP, one of the underpinnings of almost all computers networks, including the Internet. These 2 protocols are the result of the US Department of Defense funding and research. Linus Tech Tips released a great video (~4min) explaining them.

  6. Japan in the 1960s distrusted foreigners, but they wanted to build railways, so they contracted experts to teach their own people how to do it. Japan later lead the world in kilometers of high-speed rail for 30 years.
